My startup journey breeched Burlington before the remoteness of Redmond brought the misty dream to a close. I miss Clam Chowder, Cannondale, and the Cascades.

Think about it.

Modeling humans in software is easier now than modeling software in humans.

That's exactly why we should be optimistic. We have data on people. The data we have on software is already being used to eliminate humans under the guise of optimization - the same trope we've eliminated troves of jobs with for a long time.

Humans who understand cash over computers are trying to turn coders into human robots exactly as they did with HR. Emotion matters as much in code as it did in empathy. Emotion may be the only thing separating human consciousness from oversimplified machine intelligence found in IQ. Sigh.

The consequences are banal.
