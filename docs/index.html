We need softer humans, not harder software.

Hence, model humans in software, not software in humans.

We have data on people, no matter the intent of those who gathered it.

We have data on software too. But that data has already been weaponized to purge people from tech.

We need more people in tech, not less... empowered by tech and tech leadership.

We should aim AI as a tool at building better leading, better learning, and better people.

From diet to health and nutrition to school, kids and elders alike benefit when we make people the point of learning, not machines.

What the world needs now is better well-being.

Humans who understand cash better than computers have tried to turn coders into human robots exactly as we've seen with HR, without their consent.

Emotion matters as much in code as it did in empathy.

Why?

Because software is emotional communication like music. Remove the emotion and you get music made with machines while we wait for the open-hearted part that Geddy Lee promised as he sung Neil Peart's lyric in 1980.

The Greeks saw this at the Quadrivium, where they realized the connection between math and music.

Emotion may be the only thing separating human consciousness from oversimplified machine intelligence found in AI and IQ. They share the same over-used word: intelligence.

Eventually we'll realize the problem is dramatically deeper than AI or IQ. Hopefully, history will record our development of a lossy proxy like IQ to the age when we thought AI was the solution. Sigh.

The consequences are obvious.

Actual Intelligence > artificial intelligence

If we envision AI as tools instead of trauma, we may be the benefactors human:computer sensemaking revolution, who learn, grow, and thrive.

Care, oversight, and structured interlocution are required from kids to elders.

Structured interlocution = teachable moments

People who value community over commerce may benefit from the scale that commerce more naturally provides. People feel care in cooperation. People don't need cognition to feel care.
